01 — Featured Project
Capstone · Aug 2022 – May 2023 · Texas A&M

N-ICE Kart

Full Electric Go-Kart · Embedded Systems · Fabrication

A two-semester, ground-up engineering capstone — from a blank whiteboard to a fully operational electric go-kart that completed a live test drive. Every subsystem was designed, built, and validated in-house: mechanical chassis, electrical architecture, embedded sensor software, battery management, and hydraulic brakes.

Semester 01 — Design
Planning & Engineering
  • Project management, budgeting & scheduling
  • Full CAD model in SolidWorks
  • Circuit design & simulation in NI Multisim
  • System schematics & wiring diagrams
  • BMS architecture planning
  • Sensor integration design
Semester 02 — Build
Fabrication & Validation
  • MIG weldment of full chassis frame
  • Custom wire harness — fabricated by hand
  • Battery cells hand-soldered into packs
  • BMS wired and validated
  • Pneumatic brake line assembly
  • DC motor integration & test drive ✓

At the center: a Raspberry Pi running embedded Python, collecting real-time sensor data via I2C protocol from nodes distributed throughout the kart — speed, temperature, and cell voltage — displayed live on an operator LCD. The custom wire harness connecting every node was hand-fabricated and routed, verified before power-on. The entire build was lab-prototyped with oscilloscopes, DMMs, and bench power supplies before final assembly.
